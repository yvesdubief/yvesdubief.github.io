M2CE Lab

The Multiscale Mechanics and Computational Engineering Lab uses high fidelity simulations and computational models to investigate complex physics in fluid mechanics and heat transfer. It is where Elasto-Inertial Turbulence was numerically found.

Research

Project

Elasto-Inertial Turbulence and Polymer Drag Reduction

Our research group has built unique expertise in the simulation of viscoelastic flows, leading to the (re-)discovery of elasto-inertial turbulence (EIT), and, breakthrough advances in the understanding of polymer drag reduction (PDR) and (EIT). Toms (1949) discovered that dissolving minute amounts of polymers in a turbulent pipe flows could produce considerable reduction in friction drag (up to 80%). Our simulations enabled the derivation of the first conceptual model of the PDR mechanism. The model distinguished two phenomena: one causing vortices to be weakened, the other being a reinjection of energy to the flow. The latter was then speculated to be responsible for the state of maximum drag reduction, the asymptotic of PDR that is not laminar and cannot be surpassed by increasing polymer concentration.

In searching to clarify the dynamics behing the reinjection of energy, our simulations discovered a new set of coherent structures and a new state of turbulence, EIT. A recent literature review revealed that this state had been observed nearly a century ago, but had been forgotten by the scientific community. Since the first simulation of EIT, our research group along with great collaborators from University of Liege, University of Cambridge, University of Edinburgh, Institute of Science and Technology (Austria), Monash University, Weizmann Institute has been a leader in the simulation of EIT and the discovery of its properties. EIT's dynamics does not fit any existing theories of traditional turbulence and, as such, requires careful choices of numerical methods.
